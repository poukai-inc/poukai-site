## 1. Purpose

`/404` is pouk.ai's salvage surface. A visitor lands here by mistyping a URL, following a stale link, or hitting a path that no longer exists. The page's job is to (a) make the routing failure legible without degrading brand register, (b) acknowledge the dead end in brand-voice declarative tone, (c) hand the visitor back into the canonical funnel via the existing `SiteShell` nav and a single CTA back to `/`. It is the only page on the site whose KPI is *not* dwell time or conversion — it is *exit-to-canonical-route within ten seconds*. A perfect `/404` is a page nobody remembers visiting, because they re-entered the funnel cleanly and converted on another surface.

The page also carries a secondary brand contract: an error path is the surface most likely to expose carelessness. A pouk.ai-shaped `/404` proves the brand's restraint is not selective — every surface, including the broken ones, holds the same register.

## 2. Audience

- **Primary**: A visitor who intended to read pouk.ai content and landed on a non-existent path. Most common sources: a typo in the URL bar (`pouk.ai/principle` instead of `/principles`), a stale third-party link (a blog post or DM pointing to a path that was renamed), a search-engine cache lag pointing to a path that briefly existed and was removed.
- **Secondary**: A crawler hitting a non-existent path. The page must return HTTP 404 (server-side concern; Astro + Vercel handle this automatically for the static fallback) and emit `noindex` so the surface is not added to the search index.
- **Tertiary**: A curious visitor explicitly typing `pouk.ai/404` to see what the brand does on its error page. Low volume but real; the page should reward the test as much as it serves the accidental visitor.

## 3. Success criteria

- **Behavior**: The visitor lands, registers the dead-end statement in under five seconds, clicks either a `SiteShell` nav link or the body CTA to `/` within ten seconds, and re-enters the canonical funnel on a real page. Failure to act (closing the tab from `/404` without clicking anything) is acceptable — the visitor's brand impression remains intact — but click-through is the win condition.
- **Signal**: Qualitatively — the page never gets cited (as it should not; nobody screenshots a 404). When analytics arrive, the primary read-out is the click-through-rate to any other route from `/404`. A secondary read-out is the volume of `/404` visits over time — a spike indicates broken inbound links (a referrer site, a social post, a stale email) and is a maintenance signal, not a page-quality signal.
- **Failure modes**:
  - **Tonal break.** The page lands in a register the rest of the site does not carry — playful, apologetic, marketing-flourished, illustration-heavy, or any kind of "Oops!" treatment. Re-occurs if a future revision adds personality without re-deciding the brand position.
  - **Dead-end dead end.** The page acknowledges the failure but offers no path forward — no nav, no CTA, no home link. Re-occurs if the `SiteShell` chrome is omitted in the name of minimalism.
  - **Indexed by search.** A search engine treats `/404` as a canonical page and surfaces it for queries like "pouk.ai page not found." Mitigated by §6's `noindex` meta + sitemap exclusion.
  - **Sitewide refresh trigger.** A future contributor reads `/404` as a register experiment and pulls its choices forward to other pages. Mitigated by §10's explicit out-of-scope clause: `/404` is class-conforming, not class-defining.
  - **Heavy.** The page acquires illustrations, animations, card grids, search inputs, or any moves that add weight to a surface whose job is to be quick. (Note: the body link list per §4 IA item 3 is in-scope per founder override; "card grid" remains out-of-scope — the list is a typographic affordance, not a visual one.) Mitigated by §4's IA cap and §8's zero-JS AC.

## 4. Information architecture

The page composes inside existing `@poukai-inc/ui` primitives — no DS-gap, no new molecule, no v2-register moves. The compositional rhythm is the brand's v1 vocabulary: typographic restraint on `--bg`. Designer composition revision at `meta/compositions/pages/404.md` is the load-bearing artifact for exact composition primitives, type scales, surface tiers, and spacing — this spec defines the *structure and intent*; the composition lands the *recipe*. The body link list per item 3 below is the only IA unit added by the founder override on 2026-05-18 evening.

1. **`<SiteShell>`** — top nav + hairline footer chrome. Nav order per A4 / D-13: `Why AI → Roles → Principles → About`. **No nav item is marked current on `/404`.** The wordmark links to `/` (unchanged from every other page). The footer link order matches nav.
2. **`<Hero>`** — the page's primary body unit. Composes inside `<Hero>` at the existing v1 scale (`size="default"` or whichever scale the home composition uses post-DS 0.9.0; this is engineer-mechanical against the shipped DS API).
   - **Eyebrow** (optional): A short label that names the page's function. Examples for content-drafter direction (not literal copy): "404" or "Not found". Brand-voice declarative.
   - **`<h1>` title**: The page's load-bearing line. One short statement that names the dead end without theatre. Brand-voice declarative. ≤8 words. Carries the page's single `<h1>` (WCAG 1.3.1 + 2.4.6 compliance).
   - **Lede**: One sentence, body type, naming the resolution path (re-enter via nav, or return to home). ≤25 words. Single sentence; no comma-spliced two-clauses.
   - **CTA**: Single muted line, `<a href="/">` returning to home. Wording is content-drafter's lane; intended register is brand-voice declarative, restrained, differentiated from `/principles`'s and `/about`'s end-CTAs (which are `mailto:` lines — `/404`'s CTA points to `/`, not to email, because the surface's job is re-entry to the funnel, not conversion).
   - **No `status` slot.** Per Q4 above, `<StatusBadge>` stays `/`-only. `/404` does not break D-13.
   - **No `illustration` slot.** Per Q7 above, `/404` is class-conforming on v1 register — no figural imagery.
3. **Body link list** — *added per founder override 2026-05-18 evening (§0 row 3 + override audit log).* Sits **below the Hero CTA** and above the `SiteShell` footer. Three to four rows; each row pairs a route with a short descriptor (≤6 words per descriptor). Routes are drawn from the canonical funnel: `/why-ai`, `/roles`, `/principles`, `/about`. The list duplicates the `SiteShell` nav's destinations on purpose — it is a second-chance affordance for the disoriented visitor who does not scan the top nav. Semantic markup: wrap in `<nav aria-label="…">` (or `<ul>` inside an `<aside>` — designer composition picks the exact element; the screen-reader contract is that the list registers as a navigation landmark distinct from the global `SiteShell` nav). No card visuals, no icons (other than what `@poukai-inc/ui` default link styling provides), no surface-tier shifts — typographic list on `--bg`. Designer composition revision at `meta/compositions/pages/404.md` is the load-bearing artifact for exact list treatment (inline rows vs. stacked, descriptor placement, hairline rules between rows, hover treatment).
4. **`<SiteShell>` footer** — global chrome, unchanged from every other page.

**Total compositional units**: 4 (SiteShell top + Hero + body link list + SiteShell footer). Hero is the page's primary body unit; the link list is the secondary routing surface. Word-count target: **≤75 words total body content** across eyebrow + `<h1>` + lede + CTA + body link list rows (route labels + descriptors). The cap bumped from ≤50 to ≤75 to admit the list's 3–4 rows × (route label + ≤6-word descriptor) ≈ ~25 words.

**Composition density**: matches `/`'s holding-page restraint. The page is one viewport tall at typical desktop heights; the Hero + CTA sit above the fold; the body link list sits at or near the fold (designer composition decides exact placement). No scroll required to reach the CTA; the list may require a short scroll on small mobile viewports.

## 5. Content requirements

The substance is drafted by `pouk-ai-content` after this spec lands. Outcomes the copy must hit:

- **Voice contract**: Brand-voice declarative throughout. No first-person "I", no "we're sorry", no "oops", no "page not found in our database" style technical-leak, no apology theatre. The page acknowledges the dead end without performing remorse. Differentiated from `/about` v2's display-statement register (no display-scale type) and from `/principles`'s editorial-bookend register (no Instrument Serif italic body).
- **`<h1>` title**: One short statement (≤8 words) that names the dead end. Example shape (not literal — content-drafter authors): *"This page doesn't exist."* / *"Nothing here."* / *"Wrong turn."* Final wording is content-drafter authored, Arian-approved. Constraint: brand-voice declarative; no exclamation; no comma; no hedge ("It looks like…"); no metaphor stretch ("Lost in the woods"). The line lives or dies on the verb choice — *doesn't exist* is honest, *can't be found* is hedge, *was removed* is over-specific without provenance.
- **Lede**: One sentence (≤25 words). Names the resolution path. Example shape (not literal — content-drafter authors): *"Try the nav above, or head back to the homepage."* Final wording is content-drafter authored. Brand-voice declarative.
- **CTA**: Single muted line (`<a href="/">`). Wording is content-drafter's lane. Intended register: differentiated from `/principles`'s and `/about` v2's end-CTAs in two ways — (a) it points to `/`, not to `mailto:hello@pouk.ai`; (b) it is shorter (a single anchor word/phrase, not a conditional sentence). Example shape (not literal): *"Back to pouk.ai"* / *"Home"* / *"pouk.ai"*. Final wording is content-drafter authored.
- **Eyebrow (optional)**: ≤4 chars/words. Names the page's function. Brand-voice declarative. Content-drafter may drop the eyebrow if `<h1>` reads cleaner without it.
- **Body link list** *(added per founder override 2026-05-18)*: 3–4 rows surfacing canonical routes. Each row pairs a route label (the route's display name) with a short descriptor (**≤6 words**) explaining what the visitor will find there. Routes drawn from: `/why-ai`, `/roles`, `/principles`, `/about`. Recommended subset: **all four** (the page is short enough to admit them) — content-drafter may drop to three if one route reads as off-register for the disoriented-visitor audience (e.g., `/principles` may feel heavy for a salvage surface; content-drafter's call). Descriptor register: brand-voice declarative, mirrors `/`-hero's terseness — name the substance, not the verb. Example shapes (not literal — content-drafter authors): *"The AI thesis."* / *"Four shapes of work."* / *"Operating compass."* / *"The operator."* Final wording is content-drafter authored, Arian-approved. Constraint: no exclamation; no "Click here"; no "Read more"; no marketing verbs ("Discover", "Explore", "Learn"). The descriptor is a substance preview, not a call-to-action.
- **No `Draft:` example copy is locked in this section.** The copy is short enough and load-bearing enough that anchoring it to PM example phrasing risks pulling the drafter's voice off-register.

## 6. Content data shape — meta surfaces + crawler contract

`/404` reads no `src/content/*.json` file — its body is hardcoded in the page template (single-author, 3–4 line page; promoting to JSON earns no maintenance benefit). The page-level meta surfaces below are the page's data shape.

- **`<title>`**: `Page not found — pouk.ai` (separator is content-drafter's call between em-dash and pipe; match the sitewide precedent). Function-named per the `/about` precedent (A14 v1, holds in v2). The 404 status itself is not surfaced in the title text — function-named takes the slot.
- **`<meta name="description">`**: Brand-voice, ≤155 characters per agent §5.2. Names the dead end and the resolution path. Example shape (not literal — content-drafter authors): *"This page doesn't exist on pouk.ai. Use the nav above, or head back to the homepage."* (~85 chars; comfortable headroom.) Final wording is content-drafter authored.
- **`<meta name="robots" content="noindex, follow">`**: REQUIRED. `noindex` prevents the page from appearing in search results. `follow` lets crawlers traverse the nav links to canonical routes (the page is a dead-end *destination* for crawlers, but the links it carries are still useful for crawl-graph discovery).
- **OG title**: matches `<title>`.
- **OG description**: matches `<meta description>`.
- **OG image**: **reuses the existing `public/og.png`.** No `/404`-specific OG card. Social-share previews of `/404` are extremely low-volume; the brand identity stays with the wordmark-bearing default card.
- **Canonical URL**: **None.** No `<link rel="canonical">` on `/404`. Adding a canonical to an error page either points at itself (which is what `noindex` already establishes) or points at `/` (which is a deceptive crawler signal). Omitting the tag is the correct move.
- **HTTP status code**: **404.** Astro + Vercel handle this automatically for `src/pages/404.astro` rendered as the static fallback. Engineer verifies the served response is HTTP 404, not 200, at the canonical preview URL before promoting to production.
- **JSON-LD**: **None.** Per Q6 above, no structured data on `/404`.
- **Sitemap**: **`/404` is NOT listed in `sitemap.xml`.** Engineer confirms `sitemap.xml` lists exactly the five canonical routes (`/`, `/why-ai`, `/roles`, `/principles`, `/about`) post-`/about` ship; `/404` is absent.
- **Heading hierarchy**: Exactly one `<h1>` per WCAG 1.3.1 + 2.4.6. The `<h1>` is the Hero title (§4 IA item 2). No `<h2>` anywhere on the page (single-unit body; no sub-sections to rank).

## 7. User flow

- **Entry**: Three sources — (a) typo'd URL bar entry (e.g., `pouk.ai/principle` instead of `/principles`); (b) stale third-party link (a referrer site, a DM, a cached search-engine result pointing at a path that was renamed or never existed); (c) a curious visitor explicitly testing `pouk.ai/404`. The page has no entry from the canonical funnel — every nav link, footer link, CTA, and `mailto:` on the site points to real routes.
- **Read path**: `<h1>` (5-second register) → lede (5 seconds) → either click a `SiteShell` nav link (the canonical re-entry path) or click the body CTA to `/`. Total target: **≤10 seconds.** A first-touch reader resolves in seconds; there is no compositional reason to linger.
- **Exit / conversion**: Two valid exits — (a) re-enter the canonical funnel via the `SiteShell` nav (the most common exit, because the nav is the same nav the visitor was already aiming for); (b) click the body CTA back to `/`. A third *non*-exit — closing the tab — is acceptable but is not a win condition (the visitor leaves with a neutral brand impression and may re-attempt later).
- **Re-entry expectation**: Once on a canonical route, the visitor's normal flow applies (`visitor-to-conversation.md`). `/404` does not modify the canonical funnel; it salvages misaddressed traffic back into it.

## 10. Out of scope

- **A search input on `/404` (still out).** No content corpus to search at five pages; adding search would force a client-JS island and an index manifest for marginal gain. Re-evaluate the day pouk.ai grows a content surface (`/writing`, `/case-studies`) that justifies search.
- **A card grid for the body routing affordance (still out).** The body link list per §4 IA item 3 is **typographic** — a list of rows on `--bg`, no surface-tier shift, no `--surface` cards, no hairline-bordered tiles, no icons. The founder override admitted the *list*, not the *grid*. Promoting the list to a card grid is a register break and requires a new spec decision.
- **A "back" button or browser-history affordance (still out).** The browser already provides back-navigation; replicating it on-page is a UX anti-pattern. The `SiteShell` wordmark links to `/`, the Hero CTA points to `/`, and the body link list per §4 IA item 3 surfaces canonical routes — three sufficient re-entry paths. A fourth "back" affordance adds weight without function.
- **Illustrations, animations, ASCII art, lottie files, animated SVGs, "404 mascot" treatments.** The page is class-conforming, not class-defining; figural imagery on `/404` would establish a register the rest of the site does not carry.
- **The `/about` v2 register moves** (saturated orange band, display-scale type, full-bleed composition, editorial portrait, `--bg-warm-accent` token, `--fs-display-lg` consumption). `/404` is v1-register. Future cross-page register propagation per `/about` v2 §11's "cross-page revisions to `/`, `/why-ai`, `/roles`, `/principles`" clause does NOT include `/404` — error pages stay restrained.
- **A `<StatusBadge>` on `/404`.** Per D-13 and Q4 above.
- **A LinkedIn / X / GitHub / social URL** in the body, footer, or alt text. Contact mediation stays through the canonical funnel.
- **A contact form, scheduling widget, intro questionnaire, or any non-`mailto:` conversion path.** `/404` is a salvage page, not a conversion surface. No CTA points to `mailto:hello@pouk.ai` (the CTA points to `/`); the email is reached only after the visitor re-enters the canonical funnel and lands on a route that surfaces it (`/principles`, `/about`, or the SiteShell footer).
- **A "report a broken link" affordance.** Adds a form / mailto / island for low-volume signal; the inbound-email path already surfaces broken-link reports organically.
- **A per-error-type variant** (different copy for "removed", "moved", "typo'd"). The page does not know the visitor's intent; a single uniform treatment is the correct posture.
- **Translation into other languages.** English-only, matching every other route.
- **An auto-redirect after N seconds** to `/`. Auto-redirects on error pages are a dark pattern; the visitor chooses the next route.
- **Analytics-driven 404 logging beyond Matomo's default 404-event capture.** Bugsink + Matomo's defaults are sufficient at current inbound volume. Re-evaluate when 404 volume warrants a dedicated dashboard.
- **A `Cmd+K` command palette or keyboard-driven route-jumper.** Off-register for the brand; would force client-JS for the salvage surface.
- **An archived list of "pages that used to exist here."** No archive surface today; the site's route history is short.
- **A `/410` (Gone) treatment for paths that previously existed.** All non-matching paths return 404, including formerly-existing routes. Promote to a distinct `/410` treatment only if pouk.ai ever removes a previously-indexed route and wants to signal permanence to search crawlers — not the case today.
