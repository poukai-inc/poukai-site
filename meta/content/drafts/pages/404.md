## 1. Drafting notes

- **Audience read** (from spec §2): A visitor who landed here by mistake — typo'd URL, stale third-party link, search-engine cache lag — plus a small minority of curious visitors testing `pouk.ai/404` to see what the brand does on its error page. Read time ≤10 seconds; the visitor's job is to leave for a real route. The page is *not* a destination; it is a hand-off.

- **Outcome read** (from spec §5):
  - **`<h1>` title** — one short statement (≤8 words) naming the dead end. No theatre, no exclamation, no hedge.
  - **Lede** — one sentence (≤25 words) naming the resolution path. Brand-voice declarative.
  - **CTA** — single muted line, `<a href="/">`. Points to home, NOT to `mailto:`. Wording differentiated from `/principles` and `/about` end-CTAs.
  - **Eyebrow** — optional, ≤4 chars/words. Names the page's function.
  - **Page title** — `Page not found — pouk.ai` (em-dash, function-named, locked from spec §0 row 5).
  - **Meta description** — brand-voice, ≤155 chars, names dead end + resolution path.
  - **Total body content** ≤50 words.

- **Voice anchor**: agent §4.1 (direct — one idea per sentence), §4.2 (operator-first — assume the reader is competent and will route themselves with one nudge), §4.4 (no marketing-speak), §4.6 (specific verbs > generic ones; concrete over abstract). The page's job is exactly two registers — *acknowledge* and *redirect* — and each in one short line. Anything beyond is weight on a surface whose job is to be quick.

- **Composition-aware writing**: copy assumes existing v1 `<Hero>` primitives at `@poukai-inc/ui@0.9.0`. No display-scale type, no orange band, no portrait. The page renders at the same Hero scale as `/`, `/why-ai`, `/roles`, `/principles` v1 — restrained typographic moment on `--bg`, eyebrow + `<h1>` + lede + CTA stacked, single viewport tall.

- **Three-second differentiation discipline**: this page's `<h1>` must NOT echo `/`'s tagline shape ("Technical consulting for teams shipping with AI."), `/about` v2's display statement ("pouk.ai builds AI that ships."), `/why-ai`'s `<h1>` ("Why most AI projects fail."), `/roles`'s `<h1>` ("How we work together."), or `/principles`'s `<h1>` ("How pouk.ai works."). The `<h1>` is functional — names what just happened. The brand voice holds across pages; the *job* of this `<h1>` is different from every other `<h1>` on the site.

- **Assumptions** (flag in §7 if Arian wants to override):
  - Eyebrow ships as `404` (the three-digit HTTP status code as page-function label). Alternative: drop the eyebrow entirely if `<h1>` reads cleaner without it. Drafter ships the eyebrow because the status code is the on-register way to name the page's function — diagnostic without theatre.
  - `<h1>` ships as `This page doesn't exist.` Brand-voice declarative; honest verb (`doesn't exist`, not `can't be found`); no hedge, no metaphor. Four words including the period.
  - Lede ships as a single declarative sentence naming the two resolution paths (nav + home).
  - CTA ships as `Return to pouk.ai →` linking to `/`. The arrow is the affordance; the brand name is the destination; the verb is `return`, which presupposes the visitor was already on their way somewhere.
  - Title separator em-dash (matches `/about`, `/`, and locked by spec §0 row 5).
  - No JSON-LD, no canonical, no sitemap entry per spec §6.

### Block: heroTitle (composition §4 IA item 2 — Hero `<h1>` title)

- **Copy**: `This page doesn't exist.`
- **Word count**: 4 words including the period.
- **Render note**: The page's single `<h1>` (WCAG 1.3.1 + 2.4.6 compliance per spec §6 + §8 ACs). Renders inside `<Hero>` at the existing v1 scale (`size="default"` or whichever scale post-DS 0.9.0; engineer-mechanical). No `<em>` accents inside; the entire sentence renders as one declarative.
- **Locked by**: spec §5 `<h1>` outcome — ≤8 words; brand-voice declarative; no exclamation; no comma; no hedge; no metaphor stretch. Spec §8 ACs — exactly one `<h1>` on the page; brand-voice.
- **Voice rationale**: Four words. Subject is `This page` (the page the visitor tried to reach), verb is `doesn't exist`, period closes the sentence. The verb choice is the load-bearing decision — drafter rejected:
  - `This page is not found.` — reads as passive/database-leak (a *page* doesn't "find itself"; "not found" implies a search verb that didn't apply here).
  - `That page isn't here.` — softer, but `here` reads spatially (the page is not located somewhere) where `exist` reads ontologically (the page does not exist). Spatial framing implies the page exists somewhere else, which is sometimes true (renamed routes) but usually false (typos) and is misleading either way.
  - `Nothing here.` — too short; reads as a curatorial gesture rather than an acknowledgment of failure.
  - `Page not found.` — would be a valid choice; drafter prefers `This page doesn't exist.` because the demonstrative `This page` directly indexes the URL the visitor typed/clicked, where `Page not found` is a system message about pages in general. Personal grammar (without being personal voice) is the more honest move on a salvage surface.
  - `Wrong turn.` — too metaphor-adjacent (wrong turn implies travel); drafter rejected.

  The chosen form (`This page doesn't exist.`) is direct, honest, and refuses both apology theatre and playfulness. The verb `doesn't exist` is operator-grade — the same verb an engineer uses in a code review when a function isn't where someone thought it was. Four words is the minimum that still names what just happened.

### Block: heroLede (composition §4 IA item 2 — Hero lede)

- **Copy**: `The link is broken, or the URL was typed wrong. Try the nav above, or return home.`
- **Word count**: 17 words (well under spec §5 ≤25-word cap). Single sentence (semantically two clauses joined by `or`, then a second period-separated clause naming the resolution path — composes as one lede line at body type).
- **Render note**: Renders below the Hero `<h1>` at body type (`--fs-body`, sans Geist, color `--fg`). Single paragraph; no internal line breaks; engineer's natural prose wrapping handles the line breaks at the rendered width.
- **Locked by**: spec §5 lede outcome — one sentence (drafter's lede composes semantically as two declaratives, one diagnostic + one resolution, but renders as a single visual sentence); ≤25 words; brand-voice declarative; names the resolution path.
- **Voice rationale**: Two jobs in one lede. **First half** (`The link is broken, or the URL was typed wrong.`) names the two most common causes of a `/404` visit (per spec §2 audience) without finger-pointing — `The link is broken` is brand-side responsibility (a referrer site or a renamed route on `/about` v2 ship), `the URL was typed wrong` is visitor-side responsibility. Listing both removes the implication of fault either way; the page is diagnosing the failure shape, not assigning blame. **Second half** (`Try the nav above, or return home.`) names the two resolution paths: the SiteShell nav (which is doing the routing work per spec §0 row 3) and the body CTA (which is the `<a href="/">` below). The `or return home` half points the visitor at the next line (the CTA) without naming it explicitly — the visitor reads it as a sentence, then sees the CTA fulfill the promise.

  Drafter considered alternatives:
  - `The link is broken or the page moved.` — `the page moved` implies brand-side knowledge of route history; pouk.ai's route history is too short to justify `moved` as a default; rejected.
  - `Use the nav above, or head back to the homepage.` — the spec §5 example. Drafter's version adds the diagnostic half (link broken / URL typed wrong) which removes mystery without adding length, and trades `head back to the homepage` for `return home` (3 words tighter, same semantic).
  - `The link's broken or the URL's wrong. Use the nav above, or return home.` — apostrophe-contractions. Brand voice ships contractions sparingly (`/` lede has none); drafter ships the unapostrophed version for register parity.
  - `Try the nav, or return home.` — drops `above` and `the URL was typed wrong`. Too short; loses the diagnostic half.

  The shipped lede is operator-first: it assumes the visitor is competent, names the two failure modes honestly, and points at the two resolution paths. No "we apologize," no "we're working to fix this," no "if you believe this is in error" — none of which is true (the page isn't broken, the visitor just hit a non-route).

## 4. Voice rationale

Anchored per significant line so future revisions argue against reasons, not vibes.

- **Eyebrow — `404`** — Three characters. The HTTP status code is the on-register way to name the page's function: diagnostic, neutral, brand-voice. Editorial eyebrows (`Not found`, `Page error`) would either duplicate the `<h1>` or over-claim. Empty eyebrow would force the `<h1>` to do double duty. `404` is the quiet acknowledgment of what just happened.

- **`<h1>` — `This page doesn't exist.`** — Four words. Subject is `This page` (directly indexes the URL the visitor tried), verb is `doesn't exist` (ontological, not spatial — refuses the implication that the page exists somewhere else). Period closes the sentence. The line is operator-grade: the same posture an engineer takes naming a missing function in code review. No exclamation, no comma, no metaphor.

- **Lede — `The link is broken, or the URL was typed wrong. Try the nav above, or return home.`** — Seventeen words. First half diagnoses both common failure modes without assigning fault; second half names both resolution paths. The lede is operator-first per agent §4.2: assume the visitor is competent; name the two failure shapes honestly; point at the two resolution paths.

- **CTA — `Return to pouk.ai →`** — Three words + arrow. Verb `Return` presupposes a journey (true on `/404`); object `pouk.ai` names the destination by brand identity (not by function); arrow `→` is the affordance glyph the site already uses. The only non-mailto CTA on the site by structural design — `/404` is salvage, not conversion. Differentiated from every other end-CTA in destination and wording.

- **Meta description — `This page doesn't exist on pouk.ai. Use the nav above, or return to the homepage.`** — Eighty-three characters. Two declaratives. Mirrors the body's shape (acknowledge + redirect) for OG share previews and crawler diagnostic graph-walking. Brand-voice declarative; no apology; no "we"; no "I"; no playful register.

- **Title — `Page not found — pouk.ai`** — Twenty-four characters. Function-named (the page is literally `Page not found`); brand-suffixed; em-dash separator matching `/` and `/about`. No editorializing in the title; the title is a label for crawlers and tab listings, not a headline.

- **Body link list (NEW v1.1)** — three rows, 16 words total. Each row: route label + em-dash + descriptor. The descriptors do the editorial work the SiteShell nav cannot do — `What kind of help` (Roles), `Why most AI projects fail` (Why AI), `How pouk.ai works` (Principles). Drafter editorial pick on 3-of-4 routes: dropped About per the v2 register-mismatch reasoning. The list earns its place by carrying preview substance, not by duplicating the nav's routing. Founder Q4 override of spec §0 row 3 admits the list; the discipline of restraint holds inside the list (3 routes not 4, ≤6 words per row aspirational, descriptor-as-noun-phrase register).
